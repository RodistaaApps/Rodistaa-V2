## üìã ARCHITECTURE OVERVIEW

### Purpose
The Command Chain Architecture serves as the **highest-level organizational governance model** for Rodistaa, establishing authority hierarchy, escalation paths, override authority, and command structure across all roles.

**This architecture defines**:
- Who holds authority
- Who approves what
- Who escalates to whom
- Who supervises whom
- Who overrides whom
- What decisions each role can or cannot make
- How governance flows from field-level to HQ

---

## üìò ARCHITECTURE STRUCTURE

### Section 1: The Core Command Pyramid
- **8-Level Hierarchy**: MD ‚Üí HQ ‚Üí District ‚Üí Unit ‚Üí Operator ‚Üí Driver ‚Üí Shipper ‚Üí System
- **Authority Flow**: Top-down authority, bottom-up escalations
- **Decision Hierarchy**: Clear authority levels and override restrictions

### Section 2: Highest Authority (Managing Director)
- **Absolute Authority**: Business rules, escalations, compliance exceptions
- **Override Power**: Can override any decision at any level
- **Final Decision Authority**: No appeals beyond MD

### Section 3: HQ Admin (Central Command)
- **Operational Command Center**: Platform operations management
- **Override Authority**: Shipments, drivers, trucks, disputes
- **Franchise Management**: Payouts, performance, compliance

### Section 4: Compliance/KYC-Admin (Special Role)
- **Identity Expert**: KYC verification and fraud detection
- **Specialized Authority**: Identity verification only
- **No Operational Authority**: Cannot modify shipments or bookings

### Section 5: District Franchise Command Level
- **Regional Governance**: Commands all units in district
- **Inspection Oversight**: Audits and quality control
- **Escalation Management**: Handles medium-severity issues

### Section 6: Unit Franchise Field Command
- **Local Operations**: Manages local operations and inspections
- **Field Execution**: Physical inspections and compliance enforcement
- **Escalation Authority**: Raises issues to District

### Section 7: Operator Command Scope
- **Fleet Manager**: Onboard trucks, link drivers, manage fleet
- **Commercial Participant**: Place bids, manage ledger
- **Assignment Authority**: Assign drivers and trucks (subject to approval)

### Section 8: Driver Command Scope
- **Execution-Level Role**: Executes shipments, uploads photos, completes delivery
- **Limited Authority**: Cannot assign self or modify shipments
- **OTP Completion**: Mandatory OTP entry for delivery

### Section 9: Shipper Command Scope
- **Demand-Side Commander**: Creates bookings, accepts bids, approves drivers
- **Booking Authority**: Full control over bookings and bid acceptance
- **Privacy Restrictions**: Cannot view unmasked contact information

### Section 10: System Command (Automatic Authority)
- **Impartial Enforcer**: Automated rule enforcement
- **Auto-Actions**: Auto-finalization, auto-blocks, auto-alerts
- **Non-Negotiable Rules**: Cannot be overridden without authorization

### Section 11: Escalation Pathways
- **5 Escalation Paths**: Operational, Compliance, Fraud/Risk, Critical, KYC
- **Clear Escalation Routes**: Defined paths for each severity level
- **Timeframes**: Response times defined for each escalation tier

### Section 12: Override Hierarchy
- **2 Override Levels**: HQ Admin (operational), Managing Director (absolute)
- **Override Scope**: Clear definition of what can be overridden
- **Override Requirements**: Authorization and documentation requirements

### Section 13: Command Limitations Summary
- **7 Roles with Boundaries**: Clear limitations matrix
- **Hard Boundaries**: Cannot be bypassed
- **Complete Restriction List**: All prohibited actions defined

### Section 14: Command Chain Enforcement
- **Enforcement Mechanisms**: System-level, business logic, audit trail
- **Validation Rules**: Permission checks and authorization validation

## üìò PREAMBLE

This Lawbook defines the **non-negotiable business rules** governing Rodistaa's trade & transport ecosystem.

These rules bind **Shippers, Operators, Drivers, Franchises, Admin, and all platform users**.

They ensure:

- **Safety**
- **Compliance**
- **Transparency**
- **Operational reliability**
- **Zero fraud**
- **Zero intermediaries**
- **Zero commission**

Breaking these rules triggers **severe penalties**, including legal escalation when necessary.

**This Lawbook overrides all other business documents and is the ultimate authority for all Rodistaa operations.**

## üü• SECTION 1 ‚Äî DEFINITIONS (Legally Binding)

### 1.1 Booking
A load request created by a shipper, specifying origin, destination, cargo details, and price range.

### 1.2 Bid
An operator's quote for a booking, submitted through the platform with binding commitment.

### 1.3 Shipment
A booking that has been assigned to an operator after bid acceptance, creating a binding transport contract.

### 1.4 Operator
A verified truck owner with maximum 10 trucks, registered and KYC-verified on the platform.

### 1.5 Driver
A verified individual executing shipment operations, assigned to a specific truck and shipment.

### 1.6 Franchise
**Unit Franchise**: Licensed entity conducting inspections and compliance checks at local level.  
**District Franchise**: Licensed entity overseeing multiple Unit Franchises within a district.

### 1.7 Admin (HQ)
Central governance authority with highest-level oversight and enforcement powers.

### 1.8 Managing Director (MD)
Ultimate authority for all business rules, policy changes, and final appeals.

### 1.9 KYC
Know Your Customer verification process, including identity proof, address proof, and document validation.

### 1.10 POD (Proof of Delivery)
Documentation confirming successful delivery, including OTP verification and delivery photo.

### 1.11 Ledger
Operator's financial account balance on the platform, used for bidding fees and transactions.

### 1.12 Auto-Block
Automatic system enforcement that prevents user actions when violations are detected.

### 1.13 Strike
Franchise penalty point system, where accumulation leads to escalating consequences.

### 1.14 GPS Tracking
Real-time location monitoring of trucks during active shipments, transmitted every 60 seconds.

### 1.15 Inspection
Mandatory physical verification of truck eligibility, documents, and compliance status.

## üüß SECTION 2 ‚Äî ZERO-COMPROMISE RULES

The following are **absolute, non-negotiable business rules**.

They **cannot be changed by anyone except Managing Director, Rodistaa**.

### 2.1 Truck Eligibility

**Rule**: Trucks must meet ALL of the following criteria:

- **HGV only** (Heavy Goods Vehicle)
- **Year ‚â• 2018** (manufacturing year)
- **BS4 or BS6** (emission standard)
- **National Permit mandatory** (valid and non-expired)

**Violation**: Immediate BLOCK of truck and operator bidding access.

**No Exceptions**: This rule applies to all trucks, regardless of operator history or franchise recommendations.

---

### 2.2 Driver Identity

**Rule**: Driver must:

- Be **KYC-verified** with valid identity documents
- Have **valid driving license** (non-expired, appropriate class)
- **Not impersonate** or be replaced without shipper approval
- Match the **assigned driver** for the shipment

**Violation**: 
- **Block driver** immediately
- **Mandatory investigation** by District Franchise
- **Shipment reassignment** required if in progress

**No Exceptions**: Driver identity verification is mandatory for all shipments.

---

### 2.3 OTP Delivery Rule

**Rule**: Shipment completion requires:

- **System-generated 6-digit OTP** (valid for 24 hours)
- **Entered by assigned driver** only
- **Approved automatically** upon correct entry
- **No manual override** without HQ authorization

**Violation**: 
- Shipment remains **incomplete**
- Delivery status **not updated**
- Payment **not processed**
- **Investigation triggered** if OTP misuse suspected

**No Exceptions**: OTP is the only valid proof of delivery completion.

---

### 2.4 Ledger Integrity

**Rule**: Operator ledger **cannot go negative**. No exceptions.

**Violation**: 
- **Block bidding access** immediately
- **Require balance top-up** before any further operations
- **No credit extensions** under any circumstances

**No Exceptions**: Negative ledger is a system-level violation that cannot be overridden.

---

### 2.5 One Active Bid per Operator

**Rule**: Operators may place **one active bid per booking only**.

**Violation**: 
- **Auto-cancel second bid** immediately
- **Warning issued** to operator
- **Repeated violations** trigger temporary bidding suspension

**No Exceptions**: Multiple bids per booking are not permitted, even if first bid is withdrawn.

---

### 2.6 Price Visibility

**Rule**: Operators see **only shipper-adjusted price range**, not expected price or other bid amounts.

**Violation**: 
- **Critical UX breach**
- **Immediate system fix required**
- **Data access audit** if intentional

**No Exceptions**: Price transparency rules are absolute for fair bidding.

---

### 2.7 Tracking Rule

**Rule**: Driver app must transmit **GPS ping every 60 seconds** during active shipment.

**Missing >30 minutes** ‚Üí **Tracking Alert** triggered.

**Violation**: 
- **Shipment flagged** immediately
- **Admin notified** automatically
- **Driver contacted** for explanation
- **Investigation** if pattern detected

**No Exceptions**: Continuous tracking is mandatory for all active shipments.

---

### 2.8 No Direct Communication

**Rule**: Shipper and Operator/Driver must **never exchange real phone numbers**.

**Violation**: 
- **Mask enforced** + warning issued
- **Repeated violation** ‚Üí Suspension
- **Pattern detection** ‚Üí Permanent block

**No Exceptions**: All communication must occur through platform channels only.

---

### 2.9 Cash Payments Only

**Rule**: No online payments, UPI, bank transfers, or digital settlements. **Cash only**.

**Violation**: 
- **Immediate BLOCK** of user account
- **Investigation** for payment method violations
- **Legal escalation** if fraud suspected

**No Exceptions**: Cash-only policy is fundamental to Rodistaa's business model.

---

### 2.10 KYC Access

**Rule**: Only users with **KYC-admin role** may view full KYC data.

**Violation**: 
- **Admin misconduct** ‚Üí investigation
- **Access audit** required
- **Role revocation** if unauthorized access confirmed

**No Exceptions**: KYC data privacy is protected by role-based access control.

---

### 2.11 Auto-Block Events

**Rule**: System must **auto-block** under these conditions:

- Trucks with **expired documents**
- Trucks with **failed inspection**
- Trucks with **overdue inspection**
- Drivers with **KYC mismatch**
- Operators with **fraudulent bids**

**No human override** without HQ authorization.

**Violation**: 
- **System enforcement failure** ‚Üí critical bug
- **Manual override without authorization** ‚Üí Admin misconduct

**No Exceptions**: Auto-block rules are system-enforced and cannot be bypassed.

---

### 2.12 Franchise Cannot Override HQ

**Rule**: District and Unit Franchises **cannot modify HQ decisions**.

**Violation**: 
- **Franchise penalty** (strike)
- **Decision reverted** immediately
- **Escalation to HQ** for review

**No Exceptions**: HQ authority is absolute and cannot be overridden by franchises.

---

### 2.13 Fraud = Zero Tolerance

**Rule**: Any confirmed fraud results in:

- **Permanent block** of user
- **Legal escalation** if necessary
- **Franchise penalties** if involved
- **System-wide alert** to all roles

**No Exceptions**: Fraud has zero tolerance, no second chances.

---

### 2.14 Maximum Trucks per Operator

**Rule**: Operators may register **maximum 10 trucks** on the platform.

**Violation**: 
- **Additional trucks rejected** automatically
- **Existing trucks remain active** (grandfathered if applicable)
- **Operator notified** of limit

**No Exceptions**: 10-truck limit is absolute for all operators.

---

### 2.15 One Active Shipment per Driver

**Rule**: Drivers may have **one active shipment** at a time.

**Violation**: 
- **Second assignment rejected** automatically
- **Driver notified** of active shipment requirement
- **Operator must assign different driver**

**No Exceptions**: One active shipment per driver ensures operational focus and safety.

---

### 2.16 Booking Cancellation After Bids

**Rule**: If shipper cancels booking **after bids have been placed**, all bids are rejected with **NO refunds**.

**Violation**: 
- **Refund processing** ‚Üí system error
- **Bid fees retained** by platform
- **Shipper notified** of no-refund policy

**No Exceptions**: No-refund policy after bids is absolute.

---

### 2.17 Auto-Finalization Rule

**Rule**: If shipper is **inactive for 24 hours** after bids are placed, **lowest bid auto-finalizes**.

**Violation**: 
- **Manual finalization before 24 hours** ‚Üí not permitted
- **System override** ‚Üí requires HQ authorization

**No Exceptions**: 24-hour auto-finalization is automatic and binding.

---

### 2.18 Alternate Truck Assignment

**Rule**: Alternate truck assignment allowed **only for breakdown/accident** scenarios.

**No additional bidding fee** charged.

**Violation**: 
- **Unauthorized alternate assignment** ‚Üí investigation
- **Additional fee charged** ‚Üí refund required
- **Shipment ID must persist** ‚Üí violation if changed

**No Exceptions**: Alternate truck rules are strict and limited to emergencies.

## üü® SECTION 3 ‚Äî VIOLATION CATEGORIES & PENALTY MATRIX

Violations are classified into **5 categories** with escalating penalties.

### CATEGORY A ‚Äî Minor Violations

**Definition**: Operational inefficiencies that do not impact safety or compliance.

**Examples**:
- Delayed pickup photo (within 2 hours of scheduled time)
- Improper truck cleanliness (cosmetic only)
- Late inspection booking (within 7 days of due date)
- Minor documentation delays (within 3 days)
- Non-critical communication delays

**Penalty**:
- **Warning** issued automatically
- **Training reminder** sent
- **No account restrictions**
- **No financial penalties**

**Enforcement Authority**: Unit Franchise

**Appeal Process**: Available through District Franchise

---

### CATEGORY B ‚Äî Operational Violations

**Definition**: Violations that impact operational efficiency but do not compromise safety or compliance.

**Examples**:
- Driver delay >4 hours (without valid reason)
- Operator late driver assignment (>2 hours after bid acceptance)
- Missing POD documentation (delivered but not uploaded)
- Minor tracking anomalies (<2 hours missing)
- Incomplete shipment documentation

**Penalty**:
- **Operational penalty score** assigned
- **Temporary block possible** (24-48 hours)
- **Franchise involvement** required
- **Mandatory corrective action** plan

**Enforcement Authority**: District Franchise

**Appeal Process**: Available through HQ

**Repeat Offenses**: Escalation to Category C

---

### CATEGORY C ‚Äî Compliance Violations

**Definition**: Violations that breach compliance requirements but do not indicate fraud.

**Examples**:
- Expired documents (detected before use)
- Driver without proper KYC (detected before assignment)
- Auto-block conditions triggered
- Wrong driver assignment (corrected before pickup)
- Inspection overdue (>7 days)
- Document expiry within 30 days (not renewed)

**Penalty**:
- **Auto-block** enforced immediately
- **Mandatory re-verification** required
- **District Franchise audit** triggered
- **Account restrictions** until compliance restored
- **Strike** assigned to operator/franchise if applicable

**Enforcement Authority**: District Franchise + System Auto-Block

**Appeal Process**: Available through HQ with evidence

**Repeat Offenses**: Escalation to Category D

---

### CATEGORY D ‚Äî Major Violations

**Definition**: Serious violations indicating potential fraud, safety risks, or systematic non-compliance.

**Examples**:
- **Fake POD** (forged delivery proof)
- **Fake tracking data** (GPS spoofing or manipulation)
- **Fake inspection photos** (stolen or reused images)
- **Identity mismatch** (driver not matching KYC)
- **Bidding manipulation** (coordinated bidding fraud)
- **Document forgery** (fake permits, licenses)
- **Repeated compliance violations** (3+ Category C violations)

**Penalty**:
- **Immediate BLOCK** of user account
- **Franchise strike** (if franchise involved)
- **HQ investigation** mandatory
- **Suspension of operator or driver** (30-90 days)
- **Financial penalties** possible
- **Legal escalation** if fraud confirmed

**Enforcement Authority**: HQ only

**Appeal Process**: Available through Managing Director with full evidence

**Repeat Offenses**: Escalation to Category E

---

### CATEGORY E ‚Äî Critical Violations

**Definition**: Severe violations requiring immediate action, legal escalation, and permanent consequences.

**Examples**:
- **Intentional fraud** (confirmed fraudulent activity)
- **Collusion** (coordinated fraud between multiple parties)
- **Dangerous driving** (reported safety violations)
- **Threatening shippers** (harassment or threats)
- **Insurance or NP forgery** (forged legal documents)
- **Interference with GPS** (tampering with tracking systems)
- **Cash robbery** (theft of payment)
- **Identity theft** (using another person's identity)
- **System hacking attempts** (unauthorized system access)
- **Violence or threats** (physical harm or intimidation)

**Penalty**:
- **Permanent ban** from platform
- **Legal escalation** mandatory
- **Franchise termination** if involved
- **Operator blacklisting** (permanent)
- **Driver blacklisting** (permanent)
- **System-wide alert** to all roles
- **Police/RTO intervention** if required
- **Financial recovery** actions initiated

**Enforcement Authority**: HQ + Legal Authorities

**Appeal Process**: Not available for Category E violations

**No Exceptions**: Category E violations have zero tolerance and permanent consequences.

**Scope**:
- **Ultimate authority** for all business rules

## üü¶ SECTION 5 ‚Äî APPEALS PROCESS

### 5.1 Appeal Eligibility

**Eligible Violations**:
- Category A violations (Minor)
- Category B violations (Operational)
- Category C violations (Compliance)
- Category D violations (Major) - with full evidence

**Not Eligible**:
- Category E violations (Critical) - no appeals
- Confirmed fraud cases
- Legal escalation cases

---

### 5.2 Appeal Stages

#### Stage 1: Appeal Filing
- User files appeal within **7 days** of penalty
- Must provide **evidence** and **justification**
- Appeal submitted through platform

#### Stage 2: District Franchise Review
- District Franchise reviews appeal within **3 business days**
- May request additional evidence
- Can **uphold**, **modify**, or **forward** to HQ

#### Stage 3: HQ Review
- HQ reviews appeal within **5 business days**
- Conducts independent investigation if required
- Can **uphold**, **modify**, or **forward** to MD

#### Stage 4: Managing Director Final Decision
- MD reviews appeal within **7 business days**
- **Final decision** is binding
- No further appeals available

---

### 5.3 Appeal Rejection Conditions

Appeals are **automatically rejected** if:

- **Evidence of fraud** confirmed
- **Pattern of repeated violations** (3+ violations)
- **Tampered KYC** detected
- **GPS spoofing** confirmed
- **Document forgery** confirmed
- **Appeal filed after deadline** (7 days)
- **Insufficient evidence** provided
- **Appeal for Category E violation** (not eligible)

---

### 5.4 Appeal Outcomes

**Possible Outcomes**:
- **Penalty upheld** (original penalty maintained)
- **Penalty modified** (reduced severity or duration)
- **Penalty reversed** (violation cleared, account restored)
- **Additional investigation** (appeal triggers deeper review)

## üü™ SECTION 6 ‚Äî FRANCHISE LIABILITY RULES

### 6.1 Franchise Liability Scope

Franchises face liability for:

- **Fraudulent inspection** (approving ineligible trucks)
- **KYC mishandling** (unauthorized access or data breach)
- **Approving ineligible trucks** (violating Section 2.1)
- **Manipulating escalations** (improper escalation handling)
- **Interference with compliance** (blocking auto-blocks or violations)
- **Covering up violations** (not reporting Category D+ violations)
- **Improper driver verification** (approving unverified drivers)

---

### 6.2 Franchise Penalty System (Strike System)

#### Strike Accumulation
- **1 Strike**: Warning + mandatory training
- **2 Strikes**: Payout suspension (30 days)
- **3 Strikes**: Payout suspension (90 days) + HQ audit
- **4 Strikes**: Franchise termination process initiated

#### Strike Triggers
- **Category C violation** involving franchise ‚Üí 1 strike
- **Category D violation** involving franchise ‚Üí 2 strikes
- **Category E violation** involving franchise ‚Üí 3 strikes
- **Repeated violations** (3+ within 6 months) ‚Üí 1 strike per violation
- **HQ override** of franchise decision ‚Üí 1 strike
- **Compliance failure** (not reporting violations) ‚Üí 1 strike

#### Strike Expiration
- Strikes expire after **12 months** of clean operation
- **No violations** during expiration period required

---

### 6.3 Franchise Penalties

**Penalties include**:

- **Strike system enforcement** (as above)
- **Payout suspension** (temporary or permanent)
- **Legal action** (if fraud or criminal activity)
- **Termination from system** (permanent ban)
- **Financial penalties** (recovery of losses)
- **Reputation impact** (public notification if severe)

---

### 6.4 Franchise Termination Conditions

Franchise termination is initiated if:

- **4 Strikes** accumulated
- **Category E violation** directly involving franchise
- **Fraud confirmed** with franchise involvement
- **Repeated non-compliance** (systematic violations)
- **Legal action** required against franchise
- **Managing Director decision** (for any reason)

**Termination Process**:
1. HQ initiates termination process
2. Franchise notified with 30-day notice
3. Operations transferred to alternate franchise
4. Final audit and settlement
5. Permanent removal from system

- **Manipulate bidding** or shipment data
- **Interfere with franchise operations** improperly

---

### 7.2 Admin Violation Penalties

**Violations lead to**:

- **Immediate deactivation** of admin account
- **Mandatory HQ audit** of all admin actions
- **Possible termination** if severe
- **Legal action** if fraud or criminal activity

---

**Admins may override** only if:

- **HQ authorization** obtained
- **Justification documented** in system

---

### 7.4 Admin Audit Requirements

- **System modifications** (rule changes, block removals)
- **Franchise interactions** (escalations, approvals)
- **User account actions** (blocks, suspensions, terminations)

**Audit Frequency**:
- **Real-time logging** of all actions
- **Weekly review** by HQ
- **Monthly comprehensive audit**
- **Immediate audit** on violation detection

## üü´ SECTION 8 ‚Äî ZERO TOLERANCE FRAUD POLICY

### 8.1 Fraud Definition

**Fraud includes**:

- **Identity theft** (using another person's identity)
- **Document forgery** (fake permits, licenses, KYC)
- **Fake tracking** (GPS spoofing or manipulation)
- **Fake POD** (forged delivery proof)
- **Bidding manipulation** (coordinated fraud)
- **Payment fraud** (cash theft, payment disputes)
- **KYC fraud** (fake identity documents)
- **Inspection fraud** (fake inspection photos)
- **Collusion** (coordinated fraud between parties)
- **System manipulation** (hacking, unauthorized access)

---

### 8.2 Zero Tolerance Enforcement

**Fraud of ANY form leads to**:

- **Permanent user block** (no second chance)
- **Operator blacklisting** (permanent ban)
- **Franchise penalties** (strikes or termination)
- **Legal escalation** if required
- **System-wide alert** to all roles
- **Financial recovery** actions
- **Police/RTO intervention** if criminal

**"Second chance" does not apply to fraud.**

---

### 8.3 Fraud Detection Protocol

**Detection Methods**:
- **System auto-detection** (pattern recognition)
- **Franchise reporting** (inspection, verification)
- **User reporting** (shipper, operator complaints)
- **Admin investigation** (suspicious activity)
- **Audit findings** (compliance reviews)

**Response Time**:
- **Immediate action** on confirmed fraud
- **24-hour investigation** for suspected fraud
- **48-hour resolution** for complex cases

---

### 8.4 Fraud Prevention Measures

**Prevention includes**:
- **KYC verification** (mandatory for all users)
- **Document validation** (real-time checks)
- **GPS tracking** (continuous monitoring)
- **OTP verification** (delivery confirmation)
- **Auto-block enforcement** (system-level protection)
- **Franchise oversight** (local compliance)
- **HQ monitoring** (centralized oversight)

## üüß SECTION 9 ‚Äî LEGAL ESCALATION TRIGGERS

### 9.1 Mandatory Legal Escalation

Rodistaa **must escalate to legal authorities** if:

- **Identity theft** (confirmed use of stolen identity)
- **Forged documents** (fake permits, licenses, insurance)
- **Cash robbery** (theft of payment during delivery)
- **Threats or violence** (physical harm or intimidation)
- **Attempt to hack or manipulate systems** (unauthorized access)
- **Police or RTO intervention** required (regulatory violations)
- **Criminal activity** (any illegal activity on platform)
- **Fraudulent financial transactions** (payment fraud, ledger manipulation)

**These cases put Admin on immediate action.**

---

### 9.2 Legal Escalation Process

#### Step 1: Immediate Action
- **User account blocked** immediately
- **Evidence preserved** (screenshots, logs, documents)
- **HQ notified** within 1 hour
- **Legal team contacted** within 2 hours

#### Step 2: Investigation
- **Full investigation** conducted (24-48 hours)
- **Evidence collection** (all relevant data)
- **Witness statements** (if applicable)
- **Documentation** (complete case file)

#### Step 3: Legal Filing
- **Police complaint** filed (if criminal)
- **RTO complaint** filed (if regulatory)
- **Legal notice** sent (if civil)
- **Court filing** (if required)

#### Step 4: Follow-up
- **Case tracking** (monitor legal proceedings)
- **System updates** (update user status)
- **Internal notification** (alert all roles)
- **Documentation** (maintain case records)

---

### 9.3 Legal Escalation Authority

**Who Can Initiate**:
- **HQ Admin** (authorized personnel)
- **Managing Director** (ultimate authority)
- **Legal team** (if external)

**Who Cannot Initiate**:
- **Franchises** (must escalate to HQ)
- **Regular Admins** (must escalate to HQ)
- **Users** (must report through platform)

---

### 9.4 Legal Escalation Documentation

**Required Documentation**:
- **Incident report** (detailed description)
- **Evidence files** (screenshots, logs, documents)
- **User information** (KYC, contact details)
- **Witness statements** (if applicable)
- **System logs** (all relevant activity)
- **Financial records** (if payment-related)
- **Communication records** (platform messages)

**Documentation Retention**: **7 years** (legal requirement)
